# Parametric Compact Substation Design (SolidWorks)
*Industrial CAD & Product Engineering Project*

![Parametric Substation Animation](/assets/MCBA.gif)

## Project Overview
This project focuses on the advanced 3D modeling and structural engineering of a compact electrical substation (transformation post). Instead of a static model, the entire assembly was engineered using parametric design methodologies in **SolidWorks**, allowing for rapid prototyping and automated adjustments based on custom client specifications.

## Technical Details & Engineering Workflows

### 1. Parametric Design Architecture
* **Global Variables & Equations:** Implemented a robust network of equations and global variables within SolidWorks. This links critical dimensions (such as transformer capacity space, MV/LV switchboard clearances, and outer monoblock envelope dimensions) together.
* **Rapid Prototyping:** By altering a few master parameters, the entire 3D assembly, internal mounting brackets, and ventilation grilles dynamically rebuild themselves. This cuts drafting-to-manufacturing configuration time down by over 70%.

### 2. Mechanical & Electrical Integration
* **Clearance & Safety Compliance:** Engineered the internal layout to strictly respect minimum insulation air clearances for Medium Voltage (MV) equipment and Low Voltage (LV) distribution boards according to utility standards.
* **Thermal Considerations:** Designed structural ventilation louvers optimized for heat dissipation generated by the power transformer under full load conditions.
* **Sheet Metal & Structural Elements:** Utilized advanced Sheet Metal tools in SolidWorks to ensure that all enclosures, doors, and internal partitions are optimized for industrial CNC punching, bending, and manufacturing tolerances.

### 3. Deliverables & Output
* **Automated BOM (Bill of Materials):** The parametric nature of the assembly automatically generates exact structural material lists, hardware counts, and sheet metal cut-lists upon design regeneration.
* **STL Prototyping:** Generated optimized `.stl` mesh exports for rapid physical prototyping, scaled rendering evaluation, and client design-review alignment.
